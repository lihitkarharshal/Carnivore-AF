# Carnivore Diet Book Project

This directory contains the complete manuscript for a comprehensive, evidence-based book about the carnivore diet. The book is designed to guide readers from the evolutionary and biochemical "why" through to the practical "how" of animal-based living.

## Directory Overview

The project is structured as a series of Markdown files, each representing a chapter or key section of the book. It covers a wide range of topics, including:
- Evolutionary biology and ancestral dietary patterns.
- Comparative analysis with other popular diets (Keto, Paleo, Vegan).
- Biochemical deep-dives into anti-nutrients, hormones, and lipidology.
- Clinical success stories and myth-busting (Gout, Kidney Stones, Environment).
- Practical protocols for transitioning, cooking, and social navigation.

## Key Files

- **Table_of_Contents.md**: The master index of the book, providing links and summaries for all 16 chapters.
- **Introduction.md**: Outlines the modern metabolic health crisis and the philosophical return to animal-based living.
- **Chapter_1_Evolution.md**: Explores the Nitrogen-15 isotope evidence and human stomach pH that confirms our history as hyper-carnivores.
- **Chapter_3_AntiNutrients.md**: Explains the chemical defense mechanisms of plants (Oxalates, Lectins, Phytates).
- **Chapter_8_Cholesterol.md**: Details the **Lipid Energy Model (LEM)** and explains why high LDL in fat-adapted individuals is an energy transport mechanism rather than a disease marker.
- **Chapter_13_Transition.md**: A step-by-step 30-day protocol for new carnivores.
- **Chapter_14_Cooking.md**: Practical advice on sourcing, budget-friendly meat buying, and cooking techniques like the "Reverse Sear."

## Usage

This directory is intended to be used as a source for publishing a full-length book (approx. 50-60 pages). The content is formatted in Markdown for easy conversion to PDF, EPub, or print-ready formats. 

Future interactions should maintain the **conversational and accessible tone** established in these chapters while ensuring all scientific claims are grounded in the research cited within the text (e.g., the 2021 Harvard study, the Bellevue experiment, and the FASTER study).
